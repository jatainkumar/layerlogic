Real-time defect detection in metal additive manufacturing via multimodal deep learning

Team SteelSync · IIT Kharagpur Platinum Jubilee Innovation Challenge · Deep Tech for the World

The Problem

Laser Powder Bed Fusion (LPBF) is the dominant metal 3D printing process for aerospace, medical, and automotive parts. But micro-defects -- porosity, keyhole collapse, lack-of-fusion -- form invisibly during printing and compromise structural integrity.

Current quality control relies on post-build CT scanning: expensive, time-consuming, and unable to prevent defective layers from being buried under new material. The industry needs real-time, in-situ detection.

How It Works

LayerLogic fuses two data streams -- acoustic emissions and melt-pool imagery -- through pretrained CNN feature extractors and classical ML classifiers to output a real-time defect probability score.

Acoustic Branch

Audio .wav → Mel-Spectrogram → ResNet-18 (frozen) → 512-d features

Optical Branch

Image .jpg → Resize / Normalize → ResNet-18 (frozen) → 512-d features

Fusion + Classification

1024-d concat → PCA (90%) → XGBoost / RF / DT → Defect Score

Training Results

Evaluated with Stratified 5-Fold Cross-Validation on 500 paired samples (images + audio). Scores are reported as mean +/- standard deviation.
