Common Defects in Metal 3D Printing
Unstable laser energy creates three major quality problems during printing
Lack of Fusion
Keyhole Porosity
Surface Roughness
⚡ Too Little Energy ⚡ Too Much Energy 🌊 Unstable Melt Pool
The laser doesn't melt the powder
completely. This leaves gaps and holes
between layers, making the part weak
and porous.
LOW
The laser goes too deep and creates a
vapor pocket. When it collapses, it traps
gas bubbles inside the metal, forming
tiny spherical holes.
HIGH
The surface freezes unevenly, leaving
bumps and ridges. This reduces fatigue
life and creates stress concentration
points on the finished part.
UNSTABLE
03 / 15
All three defects are controlled by one thing: laser energy density — too low, too high, or unstable all cause problems.

Challenges in Process Monitoring
Too Slow, Too Late
• Traditional inspection happens after printing is done
• Expensive tools like micro-CT scanners and electron
microscopes are needed
• If a defect is found, the entire part is already wasted
• No way to fix problems while printing
⚠ Post-process inspection = zero chance to prevent defects
The solution must detect the physics of the process in real-time — not just check parameters
04 / 15
Heat Behaves Differently Everywhere
• A gear has thin teeth and a thick central hub
• Thin sections heat up and cool down very fast
• Thick sections hold heat much longer
• The same laser settings behave differently in each zone
• This causes unexpected defects even with 'correct'
parameters
💡 One-size-fits-all monitoring fails on real-world complex parts

Feature Extraction using Deep Learning
Letting the AI find the patterns — no manual engineering needed
Optical Pathway (Images)
Gear layer photos are fed into EfficientNetB0 or
ResNet-50
The network extracts a compact feature vector
capturing texture, edges, and surface patterns
Spatial Domain — What does the surface look like?
Acoustic Pathway (Sound)
Raw audio from the laser process is converted into
spectrogram images
The same deep networks read these images and
extract acoustic feature vectors
Time-Frequency Domain — What does the process sound like?
⚡ Combined Feature Vector → Ready for Classification
Both pathways are merged
